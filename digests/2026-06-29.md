## Top Stories

### [GLM 5.2 beats Claude in our benchmarks](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48709670)
Semgrep published benchmark results showing GLM-5.2, a Chinese open-weight model, outperforming Claude in their internal coding evaluations, and detailed the methodology behind the comparison. Several engineers in the thread confirmed strong real-world performance at a fraction of frontier pricing, while others flagged a consistent pattern: Chinese labs tend to show a wider spread between public benchmark rankings and independent evaluations designed to be harder to game. A broader argument ran through the entire discussion: with capable, stable models now available cheaply through multiple providers, perpetually chasing the latest frontier release looks increasingly hard to justify on cost alone.

### [The KIDS Act would require age checks to get online](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48706560)
The EFF details the KIDS Online Safety and Privacy Act, a US bill that would mandate age verification before accessing a broad range of internet services, effectively requiring identity submission to third-party intermediaries just to browse. Readers were uniformly skeptical, drawing a direct line from mandatory age gates to reusable surveillance infrastructure. A recurring technical argument: browser- or OS-level minor attestation could achieve the stated child-safety goal without building a national identity layer into every site visit, but that approach has not appeared in any serious version of the legislation.

### [I used Claude Code to get a second opinion on my MRI](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48708941)
A developer uploaded MRI scans to Claude Code Opus and published the full session alongside the actual radiologist report for direct comparison. Several findings matched the clinical read; others diverged in ways that would matter in practice. The thread collected 545 comments, drawing in both ML engineers impressed by the output coherence and medical practitioners who shared documented cases where LLMs produced confident but materially wrong clinical interpretations.

### [Professor denounces mass AI fraud on an exam at Brown](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48708991)
A Brown University professor documented that a large fraction of their class submitted AI-generated exam responses, identified through statistical similarity analysis and comparison with in-class performance. The published statement calls for a department-wide pivot to in-person handwritten assessments, arguing that take-home digital exams are no longer viable evaluation tools when capable language models are universally accessible. The case surfaces a structural problem with few clean solutions at scale.
