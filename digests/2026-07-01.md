## Top Stories

### [Claude Code is steganographically marking requests](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48734373)
Anthropic's Claude Code has been embedding steganographic markers into API requests, a technique the company describes as a defense against model distillation and unauthorized resellers. The discovery set off one of the week's largest threads: the harshest criticism, drawn directly from the article, is that the approach "mostly punishes the exact people who are easier to fingerprint: normal developers doing weird but legitimate things," while others questioned whether a method this coarse could plausibly stop a sophisticated adversary.

### [Claude Sonnet 5](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48736605)
Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5, its new mid-tier flagship, positioned on price between Haiku and Opus. Engineers in the thread immediately noticed an uncomfortable pareto kink: for several agentic search and computer-use benchmarks, Opus 4.8 remains both cheaper and stronger than Sonnet 5, narrowing the case for upgrading. The undercurrent was impatience: Fable has been unavailable for close to a month, and more than a few engineers said plainly that Sonnet 5 is not what they were waiting for.

### [Department of Commerce has lifted export controls on Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48740771)
The US Department of Commerce notified Anthropic that export restrictions on Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are being lifted, with access restoration promised within a day. A copy of the letter circulating on X attracted its own controversy: it was addressed to a named individual rather than to Anthropic, amplifying concern about unpredictable governance and the chilling effect the restrictions had on international users and teams during the weeks-long outage.

### [From brain waves to words: a new path to communication without surgery](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48739466)
Meta's Brain2QWERTY research demonstrates non-invasive brain-to-text decoding using magnetoencephalography, reconstructing sentences without implants. The improvement over prior work is statistically significant but incremental; the more durable contribution is releasing the full code and dataset. The practical ceiling is evident: MEG machines are room-sized and prohibitively expensive, and commenters were candid that the gap between this result and a deployable assistive device remains very large.
