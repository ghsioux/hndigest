## Top Stories

### [SpaceX says it has agreement to acquire Cursor for $60B](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47855293)
SpaceX announced an option to acquire the AI coding assistant Cursor for $60B, or pay $10B for a partnership. The move signals a push to consolidate AI talent and developer data under the xAI umbrella. The community is highly skeptical, viewing the valuation as financial engineering to boost pre-IPO hype rather than a strategic aerospace move.

### [Framework Laptop 13 Pro](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47852177)
Framework unveiled its new 13 Pro model, featuring Intel Core Ultra processors, LPCAMM2 memory, and a redesigned chassis promising 20+ hours of battery life. It positions the modular laptop as a viable MacBook Pro competitor for Linux users. Commenters are pleased by the backward compatibility—allowing old mainboards to drop into the new chassis—though some note the high cost of the new RAM standard.

### [ChatGPT Images 2.0](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47852835)
OpenAI released an upgrade to its image generation model, showcasing improvements in photorealism, prompt adherence, and text rendering. While the technical leap is clear, the mood in the comments shifted from anticipation to jaded concern about the societal impact of indistinguishable fake images and the compute cost of generating them.

### [Changes to GitHub Copilot individual plans](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47838508)
GitHub is restructuring Copilot Pro, removing access to Claude 3 Opus 4.6 and replacing it with 4.7 at a 7.5x rate multiplier, while pausing new signups. It is a stark indicator that the era of subsidized LLM inference is ending. Users feel rug-pulled, noting that the per-request billing model was fundamentally broken for agentic workflows.
