### [The EU's replaceable battery mandate has a massive loophole](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47834195)
The EU will require replaceable batteries in phones and tablets starting in 2027. However, HN commenters quickly spotted an exemption for devices whose batteries maintain 80% capacity after 1,000 cycles. Since most flagship phones already claim this metric, or can achieve it via software limits, the regulation may change very little in practice.

### [Quantum computers are not a threat to AES-128](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47836784)
Cryptographer Filippo Valsorda breaks down why Grover's algorithm is not a practical threat to 128-bit symmetric keys. While asymmetric cryptography faces real risks from Shor's algorithm, the physical and time costs of quantum symmetric attacks make them entirely infeasible.
