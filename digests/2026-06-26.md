## Top Stories

### [An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)
The Vesuvius Challenge team has achieved a complete read of a Herculaneum scroll buried by Vesuvius in 79 AD. ML models applied to micro-CT scans enabled virtual unrolling and character recognition across the entire text, a first for any scroll of this type. The discussion was celebratory, with commenters reflecting that the author writing around 200 BC could never have imagined their words surviving a volcano, let alone being decoded by a neural network two millennia later.

### [The 'papers, please' era of the internet will decimate your privacy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)
Mandatory age and identity verification laws, spreading across jurisdictions, would force users to present credentials to every website they visit. FIRE argues this fundamentally destroys online anonymity even with technically "anonymous" credential schemes. The technical thread ran deep: zero-knowledge proof protocols could theoretically satisfy regulators without exposing identity, but others countered that such systems are practically defeated by users selling their tokens for trivial compensation.

### [Apple raises prices of MacBooks, iPads](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48672732)
Apple raised prices across its Mac and iPad lineup by $100 to $300 depending on model. The move is not isolated: Microsoft simultaneously announced Xbox hardware price hikes, citing surging component costs. Commenters observed the entire consumer electronics sector is repricing in lockstep, and Apple is simply the most visible target absorbing the backlash.

### [IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967)
IBM announced a "0.7 nm" (7 angstrom) node, claiming transistor scaling can continue below the 1 nm barrier using a new nanostack architecture. The community was unconvinced: top comments explained the underlying physical features are actually around 5 nm, and that chip node names have been marketing labels disconnected from real dimensions for years. Several engineers called for the industry to measure density in logic gates per square millimeter instead.
