## Top Stories

### [An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)
The Scroll Prize team announced the first full read of a carbonized Herculaneum scroll, pairing imaging, segmentation, and machine-learning work with a public preprint and code release. HN is treating it as one of those rare wins where applied computer vision turns into a humanities breakthrough, though commenters are also digging into how much manual reconstruction remains behind the "read" headline.

### [Apple raises prices of MacBooks, iPads](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48672732)
Apple price increases tied to skyrocketing memory costs triggered a huge thread about DRAM/NAND supply, AI data-center demand, and how fast hardware margins can shift when the component market tightens. The technical angle is less about the devices themselves and more about the knock-on effects of AI infrastructure spending on consumer hardware.

### [The "papers, please" era of the internet will decimate your privacy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)
Age-verification laws and platform identity checks are again front-page material, with the HN discussion centering on the technical impossibility of proving age online without building reusable surveillance infrastructure. Commenters are skeptical of vendor promises around zero-knowledge or tokenized identity when the deployment pressure is toward centralized, app-store-style gatekeeping.

### [IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967)
IBM's sub-1 nm chip announcement sparked the usual HN split between marketing-node skepticism and genuine interest in nanosheet/GAA progress. The useful thread is about what "sub-1 nm" means in modern semiconductor branding, how far research demos are from foundry economics, and whether packaging and memory bandwidth matter more than transistor naming.
