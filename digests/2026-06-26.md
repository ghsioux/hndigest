## Top Stories

### [An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)
The Vesuvius Challenge team announced that an entire Herculaneum scroll—carbonized by the 79 AD Vesuvius eruption and unreadable for nearly two millennia—has been fully decoded using machine learning applied to X-ray CT scans. The paper reveals a lost philosophical text by Aristocreon. The HN thread reacted with wonder, with commenters marveling at the recursive improbability of silicon-based systems reconstructing ancient silicon-adjacent sand-and-lightning technology to recover thoughts no human had accessed since antiquity.

### [The 'papers, please' era of the internet will decimate your privacy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)
FIRE's essay argues that age-verification mandates sweeping across Western governments are laying the groundwork for pervasive internet ID checks. The HN discussion (300+ comments) is technically rich: cryptographers advocate anonymous credential schemes that can prove age without linkability, while skeptics point out that credential transfer among minors makes any non-biometric system unenforceable, leaving governments with a choice between useless friction and invasive surveillance.

### [IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967)
IBM announced a 0.7 nm (7 angstrom) "nanostack" architecture using complementary field-effect transistors. The HN thread quickly peeled back the marketing: actual horizontal feature sizes remain well above 1 nm and are comparable to current 5 nm processes; the sub-nanometer figure describes vertical layer thicknesses achievable for decades. Engineers in the discussion argue the industry should switch to gates-per-mm² as the canonical density metric and abandon node names entirely.

### [Apple to skip high-end M6 Mac chips in favor of AI-focused M7 line](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48676795)
Bloomberg reports Apple will bypass the M6 Pro/Max/Ultra tier and jump directly to M7, explicitly redesigning the chip hierarchy around on-device AI inference. The HN thread debates whether local-first LLMs can ever reach data-center parity on power-constrained hardware, with several engineers arguing that thermal limits and memory bandwidth will keep quantized on-device models well below A100-class clusters even as node shrinks continue.
