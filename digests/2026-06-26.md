## Top Stories

### [An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)
The Vesuvius Challenge team has achieved a milestone: full computational transcription of an entire carbonized Herculaneum scroll, using ML-based ink detection and segmentation to read text buried for roughly 2,000 years. A team member answered methodology questions directly in the comments, and readers drew a pointed contrast between this category of transformative scientific AI use and the more mundane applications dominating the current hype cycle.

### [Apple raises prices of MacBooks, iPads](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48672732)
Apple has raised prices across its MacBook and iPad lineup, with the MacBook Neo moving to $699, the 13-inch Air to $1,299, and the 15-inch Air to $1,499. With over 1,000 comments, the discussion split between attributing the hike to tariffs and memory supply constraints and questioning why one of the most profitable companies in the world couldn't absorb it in the margin.

### [The 'papers, please' era of the internet will decimate your privacy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)
Online age-verification laws and digital identity mandates are quietly building the infrastructure for pervasive web tracking, as dissected in a piece from EFF's Expression project. Readers challenged each other over whether anonymous credentials, zero-knowledge proofs, or device attestation could realistically mitigate the risk, with the sharpest disagreement reserved for "trusted computing": whether it solves the problem or poses a deeper threat to user autonomy.

### [IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967)
IBM announced a chip architecture it claims pushes below the 1-nanometer node barrier, invoking "angstrom-level scaling" as the frontier for transistor density. Commenters challenged the framing on technical grounds: scale bars on the published micrographs drew scrutiny, physical gate-length lower bounds around 10 to 15 nm were cited, and the longstanding disconnect between semiconductor node names and actual physical dimensions got another airing.
