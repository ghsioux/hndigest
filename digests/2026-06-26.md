## Top Stories

### [An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)
The Vesuvius Challenge has reached its landmark goal: researchers have decoded a complete scroll from the Herculaneum library, a collection of papyri carbonized when Vesuvius erupted in 79 AD. Machine learning models applied to X-ray CT scans detected ink patterns invisible to the naked eye, recovering coherent text across the entire roll for the first time. The thread marvels at the civilizational scale of the feat — text composed around 200 BC, destroyed by a volcano, resurrected by algorithms running on silicon made from sand.

### [IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967)
IBM announced a "0.7 nm" nanostack chip architecture, positioning it as the first demonstration that transistor scaling can continue below the 1 nm node. The sub-nanometer figure refers to vertical layer thicknesses, not gate lengths — the horizontal dimensions that govern circuit density remain closer to 5 nm, a distinction the semiconductor industry has systematically obscured by abandoning density metrics in favor of increasingly abstract node names.

### [The 'papers, please' era of the internet will decimate your privacy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)
Laws mandating online age verification are spreading globally, and FIRE argues the resulting identity infrastructure will permanently erode internet anonymity. Some commenters propose cryptographic fixes — anonymous credentials that can prove age without linking sessions across users — but skeptics counter that no technical scheme stops a minor from borrowing a parent's credential, and only real-world liability makes verification meaningful. The community is split between cryptographic optimists and those who think the policy debate has already foreclosed the privacy-preserving path.

### [Apple to skip high-end M6 Mac chips in favor of AI-focused M7 line](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48676795)
Bloomberg reports Apple plans to bypass a high-performance M6 tier entirely and jump to an AI-optimized M7 architecture, arriving alongside steep Mac price increases. The thread reads the pivot as Apple trading peak compute headroom for memory bandwidth and neural inference throughput, compounded by DRAM supply constraints that make extreme-RAM configurations economically brutal. Critics argue the company is quietly pricing out the local-AI workload narrative it spent two years building.
