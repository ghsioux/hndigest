## Top Stories

### [An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48675179)
The Vesuvius Challenge team has fully decoded a Herculaneum scroll—carbonized by the 79 AD eruption and unopenable for nearly two millennia—by combining high-resolution X-ray CT scanning, ML-based virtual unwrapping, and ink-detection models, all released open-source. The recovered text is a lost Stoic treatise on ethics that names Aristocreon, and it is the first sealed Herculaneum roll ever read end to end.

### [The 'papers, please' era of the internet will decimate your privacy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48679608)
FIRE's essay argues that the age-verification mandates spreading across Western governments are quietly building the infrastructure for pervasive internet ID checks. Commenters split along technical lines: some champion anonymous-credential schemes that can prove age without linkability, while skeptics counter that credential-sharing among minors makes any non-biometric system unenforceable—leaving only useless friction or outright surveillance.

### [IBM debuts sub-1 nanometer chip technology](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48674967)
IBM unveiled a 0.7 nm (7-angstrom) "nanostack" architecture built on complementary field-effect transistors. Critics were quick to peel back the branding: horizontal feature sizes still sit around current 5 nm processes, and the sub-nanometer figure refers to vertical layer thickness that has been achievable for years. Several engineers argue the industry should drop node names entirely in favor of gates-per-mm².

### [Apple to skip high-end M6 Mac chips in favor of AI-focused M7 line](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48676795)
Bloomberg reports Apple will skip the M6 Pro/Max/Ultra tier and jump straight to an M7 line redesigned around on-device AI inference. The debate that followed centered on whether power-constrained, local-first LLMs can ever approach data-center parity, with several engineers arguing that thermal ceilings and memory bandwidth will keep quantized on-device models well below A100-class clusters even as nodes keep shrinking.
