## Top Stories

### [I believe there are entire companies right now under AI psychosis](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48153379)
HashiCorp founder Mitchell Hashimoto coined "AI psychosis" to describe companies shipping buggy code under the delusion that AI agents will easily fix it later. The HN thread is a goldmine of anecdotes about management forcing top-down "spec-driven development" and the loss of engineering principles. The top insight: being fast at fixing bugs is irrelevant if the ratio of defects introduced to defects fixed remains above one.

### [A 0-click exploit chain for the Pixel 10](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48148460)
Project Zero published a detailed write-up of a 0-click exploit chain targeting a driver bug in the Pixel 10. The community noted a dark irony: the vulnerable code was introduced specifically to support new AI features that decode media for analysis before the user even opens a message. It is a stark reminder that adding complex, automated processing to messaging apps drastically increases the 0-click attack surface.

### [The sigmoids won't save you](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48147021)
Scott Alexander argues against the common assumption that AI progress will inevitably flatten into a sigmoid curve soon. He suggests that without a specific model, we should default to Lindy's Law—expecting a trend to continue as long as it already has. Commenters pushed back, noting that technological progress is rarely a single curve, but rather a series of "stacked sigmoids" driven by unpredictable breakthroughs.
