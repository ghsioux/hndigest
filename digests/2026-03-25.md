## Top Stories

### [Goodbye to Sora](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47508246)
OpenAI is shutting down its Sora video generation app and exiting its $1B deal with Disney. The move signals a major strategic pivot away from consumer entertainment and towards B2B coding and enterprise tools. Commenters note the underlying reality: generating video is incredibly compute-intensive, difficult to monetize, and fraught with IP issues, making an "AI TikTok" a poor use of capital when Anthropic is eating their lunch in the enterprise space.

### [Tell HN: Litellm 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 on PyPI are compromised](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47501426)
A severe supply chain attack hit the popular `litellm` Python package after the maintainer's account was compromised. The malicious payload steals credentials and crypto wallets, and uses a novel `.pth` file technique to execute code on module discovery rather than waiting for an import. The attackers also deployed bots to flood the GitHub issue with fake positive comments to stifle discussion and delay remediation.

### [Wine 11 rewrites how Linux runs Windows games at kernel with massive speed gains](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47507150)
Wine 11 introduces NTSYNC, moving Windows synchronization primitives directly into the Linux kernel rather than emulating them in userspace. This architectural shift brings massive performance leaps for gaming. While the headline 8x speedups are compared to vanilla Wine (without existing fsync/esync workarounds), the community highlights this as a monumental engineering achievement that perfectly recreates NT kernel semantics on Linux.
