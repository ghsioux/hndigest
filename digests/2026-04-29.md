## Top Stories

### [Ghostty is leaving GitHub](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47939579)
Mitchell Hashimoto announced his popular terminal emulator is migrating off GitHub due to constant outages (94 incidents in 90 days) and the platform's shift toward "vibe coding." The post struck a deep chord on HN, with many commenters lamenting the decline of GitHub's reliability post-Microsoft acquisition and discussing the need for federated alternatives.

### [An update on GitHub availability](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47932422)
In response to mounting criticism, GitHub's CTO published a post blaming the recent instability on the exponential growth of "agentic development workflows" overwhelming their infrastructure. They claim to be prioritizing availability and moving to a multi-cloud setup. The HN community was highly skeptical, criticizing the unlabelled graphs and questioning why human developers must suffer degraded performance to subsidize AI agent traffic.

### [GitHub RCE Vulnerability: CVE-2026-3854 Breakdown](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47936479)
Wiz researchers uncovered a critical remote code execution vulnerability in GitHub Enterprise Server caused by a simple command injection in `babeld` via `git push -o` options. Alarmingly, 88% of on-prem instances remain vulnerable weeks after the patch. The discovery highlights how AI is accelerating reverse engineering and vulnerability discovery in binaries.

### [Your phone is about to stop being yours](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47935853)
Google is pushing a mandatory update in September 2026 that will block all Android apps unless the developer registers, pays a fee, and provides government ID. While an "advanced flow" opt-out exists, it requires a 24-hour cooling-off period. Commenters view this as the final nail in the coffin for Android's promise of openness, effectively killing casual sideloading and pushing the ecosystem closer to Apple's walled garden.
