### [Bun's experimental Rust rewrite hits 99.8% test compatibility](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48073680)
Bun's creator used Claude to rewrite the massive Zig codebase into Rust in just 6 days, hitting near-perfect compatibility on Linux. The community is split between awe at the speed and skepticism about the maintainability of a million lines of "vibe-coded" Rust, while also debating what this means for Zig's future.

### [A recent experience with ChatGPT 5.5 Pro](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48071262)
Fields Medalist Timothy Gowers shared how ChatGPT 5.5 Pro solved a non-trivial combinatorial mathematics problem that would normally be a solid PhD thesis chapter. The thread is a philosophical reflection on the future of human achievement, with some mourning the loss of "immortality" in math research, while others argue humans will simply shift to guiding and verifying AI discoveries.

### [Internet Archive Switzerland](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48074265)
A new, independent Swiss non-profit foundation has been formed to mirror and expand the Internet Archive's mission. It provides a legally and geographically distinct backup to the US-based archive, which has faced increasing legal and political pressure. Commenters welcome the resilience but note the site was immediately hugged to death, underscoring the technical challenges of decentralizing 175PB of data.

### [EU Parliamentary Research Service calls VPNs "a loophole"](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48072190)
A European Parliament research paper on age verification quoted officials calling VPNs a loophole that needs closing, signaling growing political appetite to restrict VPN usage under the guise of child safety. While many commenters express outrage at the creeping surveillance state, others point out the headline is slightly misleading—the paper was quoting a UK official, not establishing official EU policy.
