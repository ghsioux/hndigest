## Top Stories

### [Artemis II safely splashes down](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47725583)
The Artemis II crew safely returned to Earth, completing the first crewed lunar flyby in over 50 years. The HN discussion focused on the immense engineering challenges, the relief over the heat shield holding up after previous concerns, and the stark reality of NASA's 1-in-30 acceptable mortality rate for the mission.

### [AI assistance when contributing to the Linux kernel](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47721953)
The Linux kernel project published official guidelines for using AI coding assistants. It takes a refreshingly pragmatic approach: developers can use AI, but they must review the code, ensure GPL-2.0 compliance, and take full responsibility via the `Signed-off-by` tag. Comments debated the feasibility of guaranteeing license compliance from models trained on mixed-license code.

### [WireGuard makes new Windows release following Microsoft signing resolution](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47719942)
WireGuard released a new Windows version after Microsoft restored their developer account. The account was locked by an automated system and only restored after a massive HN thread caught Microsoft's attention. The community expressed deep concern about the fragility of the Windows ecosystem for smaller open-source developers who lack the visibility to bypass automated bans.

### [CPU-Z and HWMonitor compromised](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47717847)
The official website for the popular hardware utilities CPU-Z and HWMonitor was compromised to serve malware installers. It's a classic watering hole attack targeting tech-savvy users. The thread highlighted the vulnerability of downloading binaries directly from websites and the advantage of using package managers like `winget` that perform signature checks.
