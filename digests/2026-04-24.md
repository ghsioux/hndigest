## Top Stories

### [GPT-5.5](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47879092)
OpenAI released GPT-5.5, claiming better benchmark performance and higher token efficiency. While the per-token price has increased, the model requires fewer output tokens to achieve the same results. The HN discussion is split between excitement over the efficiency gains and skepticism about the industry's trend of "benchmaxxing" and rising API costs.

### [DeepSeek v4](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47884971)
DeepSeek launched its v4 models (Pro and Flash) with open weights, delivering frontier-level performance at a fraction of the cost. Engineers in the comments are particularly impressed by the low-level optimizations, such as deterministic kernels, and the fact that the models run entirely on Huawei chips, offering a viable alternative to the CUDA ecosystem.

### [Bitwarden CLI compromised in ongoing Checkmarx supply chain campaign](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47876043)
A malicious GitHub Action in Bitwarden's CI/CD pipeline led to a compromised CLI release. While the blast radius was limited (vaults were untouched), commenters highlighted a novel attack vector: the payload targeted AI tooling configurations and shell profiles, attempting to poison the context windows of coding assistants.

### [An update on recent Claude Code quality reports](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47878905)
Anthropic published a postmortem confirming bugs that degraded Claude Code's performance, such as silently stripping "thinking" tokens after an hour of idle time to save cache costs. The community expressed frustration over the lack of transparency and prior gaslighting, underscoring the fragility of agent harnesses and the tension between cost optimization and model quality.
