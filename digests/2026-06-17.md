### [SpaceX to buy Cursor for $60B](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48553224)
SpaceX is acquiring the AI coding editor Cursor for $60B in stock. The massive valuation—roughly 15 years of future cash flows—signals xAI's aggressive push into developer tools and enterprise distribution. The HN thread is highly skeptical of the price tag for a VS Code fork, and many users are threatening to cancel their subscriptions due to the association with Elon Musk.

### [Is Meta destroying its engineering organization?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48558045)
A report claims Meta is forcefully reassigning 30-50% of engineers on core teams to data labeling and RLHF tasks. The move highlights the extreme measures companies are taking to win the AI race, treating highly paid engineers as training data mules. Commenters speculate this might be a calculated move by leadership to drive voluntary attrition without formal layoffs.

### [GrapheneOS has been ported to Android 17](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48561654)
The privacy-focused mobile OS has successfully rebased on Android 17. As Google pushes deeper, system-level AI integration into the base OS, privacy advocates are increasingly looking for alternatives. Interestingly, the discussion highlights a divide: while some dread the new AI features, others admit they want local, offline AI agents to handle sysadmin tasks on their phones.

### [Stop Killing Games fails to secure EU law despite 1.3M signatures](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48564696)
The EU Commission rejected an initiative to legally require publishers to keep games playable after commercial support ends. However, commenters clarify this was an expected setback. The initiative is now shifting focus to the European Parliament to amend the Digital Fairness Act, turning a perceived defeat into a strategic pivot.
