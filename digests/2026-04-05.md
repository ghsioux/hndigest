### [AWS engineer reports PostgreSQL perf halved by Linux 7.0, fix may not be easy](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47644864)
A severe performance regression in the upcoming Linux 7.0 kernel halves PostgreSQL throughput on high-core ARM machines due to changes in how process preemption handles spinlocks. The immediate fix requires enabling huge pages—which many users neglect—sparking a debate over whether the kernel should break userspace performance for architectural purity.

### [German implementation of eIDAS will require an Apple/Google account to function](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47644406)
Germany's implementation of the EU's digital identity framework (eIDAS) relies on device attestation APIs that effectively mandate the use of Google or Apple accounts. The HN community is heavily criticizing the move as a surrender of European digital sovereignty to US tech giants, noting that physical smartcards already solve this without the dystopian lock-in.

### [How many products does Microsoft have named 'Copilot'?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47642569)
Microsoft's branding strategy has reached peak absurdity, with the name 'Copilot' now applied to at least 75 different apps, features, and subscriptions. Commenters compare it to the `.NET` and `Live` naming fiascos of the past, noting that the unified branding makes it nearly impossible to file coherent bug reports or discuss specific tools.

### [The threat is comfortable drift toward not understanding what you're doing](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47647788)
A thoughtful essay argues that the real danger of AI agents isn't malice, but convenience—specifically, the loss of deep expertise as junior developers use LLMs to skip the struggle of learning. The HN discussion highlights the tension between shipping products faster and the long-term atrophy of the foundational skills needed to debug complex systems.
