## Top Stories

### [Midjourney Medical](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48579650)
Midjourney announced a bizarre expansion into medical imaging, proposing "Fullbody Ultrasonic Computational Tomography" spas with a goal of a billion scans a month by 2031. The HN thread is overwhelmingly skeptical. Radiologists pan the sample images, physicists question the feasibility, and many suspect the company has fallen into "LLM psychosis" by confusing generative AI hype with hardware physics and FDA regulations.

### [GLM-5.2 is the new leading open weights model on Artificial Analysis](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48567759)
Zhipu AI released GLM-5.2, an open-weights model that benchmarks near Claude Opus 4.7 levels. While it lacks vision capabilities and the official API is melting under demand, the community confirms it is highly capable for coding. The gap between open-weights and closed frontier models is now measured in months, not years.

### [Volkswagen started blocking GrapheneOS users](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48571526)
VW's mobile app now strictly enforces Google Play Protect attestation, locking out users of GrapheneOS and other custom Android ROMs. This breaks community integrations and local API access for charge control and climate settings. The discussion highlights the growing danger of hardware that requires an app, and how Google's attestation API is effectively killing the third-party ecosystem.

### [Lore – Open source version control system designed for scalability](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48571081)
Epic Games open-sourced Lore, a Rust-based VCS designed specifically for game development to handle massive binary assets. It aims to compete with Perforce, the aging industry standard. While some users were put off by the LLM-generated documentation, game developers in the thread emphasize how desperately the industry needs a viable alternative to Perforce.
