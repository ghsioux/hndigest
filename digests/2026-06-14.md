### [Amazon CEO's talks with U.S. officials triggered crackdown on Anthropic models](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48519092)
A WSJ report claims Amazon CEO Andy Jassy's discussions with the US government led to the recent export ban on Anthropic's frontier "Fable" model. The HN community is baffled by the motives—given Amazon's massive stake in Anthropic—and deeply concerned about the precedent. Commenters note that arbitrary executive actions are destroying trust in US cloud providers, pushing developers toward open-weight alternatives.

### [Noise infusion banned from statistical products published by Census Bureau](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48517377)
The US Census Bureau has banned "differential privacy" (noise infusion) from its statistical releases. The technique was originally introduced to prevent the deanonymization of census respondents. The thread is heavily polarized: some argue the ban is a political maneuver to enable precise gerrymandering, while data scientists point out that the previous noise infusion made the data practically unusable for legitimate downstream research.

### [Every Frame Perfect](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48516251)
A critique of modern UI animations arguing that intermediate frames often break layout logic and look broken when paused. The discussion is a classic HN debate on design philosophy: purists agree that modern UIs are bloated with unnecessary, janky motion, while graphics engineers argue that evaluating still frames ignores how human vision processes motion blur and temporal continuity.
