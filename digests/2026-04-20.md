## Top Stories

### [Vercel April 2026 security incident](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47824463)
Vercel confirmed a security breach originating from a compromised third-party AI tool used by an employee, which allowed attackers to pivot through Google Workspace OAuth into Vercel's internal systems. The incident exposes the growing attack surface of interconnected SaaS tools. HN commenters expressed frustration over Vercel's vague initial communication and the fact that environment variables not explicitly marked as "sensitive" were exposed.

### [Changes in the system prompt between Claude Opus 4.6 and 4.7](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47823270)
Anthropic's massive 80k-token system prompt for Claude Opus 4.7 reveals extensive new behavioral guardrails, including strict malware paranoia and instructions to make reasonable attempts rather than asking clarifying questions. The community is split: some appreciate the reduced friction, while others complain the model now makes wild assumptions and refuses legitimate coding tasks due to overzealous safety filters.

### [The RAM shortage could last years](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47822414)
Driven by insatiable demand from AI companies hoarding hardware, the global RAM shortage is expected to persist until at least 2028. With only three major manufacturers left, the industry is hesitant to overbuild capacity for fear of an AI bubble burst. Commenters noted the irony that this might finally force developers to write more memory-efficient software, potentially spelling the end for bloated Electron apps.

### [The Bromine Chokepoint](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47826100)
A geopolitical analysis highlights how the global semiconductor supply chain relies heavily on bromine extracted from the Dead Sea, with extraction and conversion infrastructure co-located in a vulnerable conflict zone. While some HN readers dismissed this as another "running out of sand" panic, others pointed out that hyper-efficient, specialized supply chains inherently lack the redundancy needed to survive localized disruptions.
