## Top Stories

### [Google Chrome silently installs a 4 GB AI model on your device without consent](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48019219)
Chrome 148 is downloading a 4GB Gemini Nano model to users' devices by default to support the new Prompt API. The HN discussion is overwhelmingly negative, with users outraged over the storage consumption and the data usage costs for those on metered connections. Many see this as a final push to switch to Firefox or LibreWolf.

### [.de TLD offline due to DNSSEC?](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48027897)
The entire `.de` top-level domain experienced a major outage due to a botched DNSSEC key rollover by DENIC, rendering major German sites unreachable for hours. The incident sparked debates about the fragility of DNSSEC, with commenters noting the irony of a security feature causing a massive availability failure.

### [Accelerating Gemma 4: faster inference with multi-token prediction drafters](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48024540)
Google released multi-token prediction drafter models for Gemma 4, enabling speculative decoding that speeds up inference by up to 2.5x without quality degradation. The local AI community is thrilled; commenters note that Gemma 4 26B/31B models are now blazing fast on consumer hardware, rivaling Qwen's performance but with much higher efficiency.

### [Zuckerberg 'Personally Authorized and Encouraged' Meta's Copyright Infringement](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48026207)
A new lawsuit alleges Mark Zuckerberg personally directed Meta to pirate millions of books to train Llama, deliberately stripping copyright metadata and abandoning plans to license the works. Commenters are cynical about the lack of consequences for billionaires compared to individuals like Aaron Swartz, though some still defend AI training as fair use.
