## Top Stories

### [Project Glasswing: Securing critical software for the AI era](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47679121)
Anthropic announced "Claude Mythos Preview," a new frontier model they claim can autonomously discover and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in major operating systems. The accompanying [244-page system card](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47679258) shows jaw-dropping benchmark jumps, including SWE-bench Verified hitting 93.9%. However, Anthropic is not releasing the model generally, instead partnering with big tech for "defensive cybersecurity." The HN community is split: some see a massive shift in security, while others view the "too dangerous to release" narrative as a brilliant marketing tactic to inflate valuation and reserve top models for enterprise budgets.

### [Veracrypt project update](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47686549)
The VeraCrypt project reported that Microsoft disabled their developer certificate without warning, blocking new Windows releases. The thread escalated when WireGuard creator Jason Donenfeld chimed in to reveal his Microsoft signing account was also suspended without notice. The incident serves as a chilling reminder of platform control and the fragility of independent software distribution on Windows.

### [S3 Files](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47680404)
AWS announced a new feature to mount S3 buckets as a file system. While it sounds like magic, the comments quickly dissected the architecture: it's essentially an eventually consistent EFS cache sitting in front of S3. Engineers warn that the lack of atomic rename and the underlying EFS pricing model make it a potentially expensive and risky drop-in replacement for true POSIX file systems.
