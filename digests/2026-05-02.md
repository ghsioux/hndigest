## Top Stories

### [The gay jailbreak technique](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47977134)
A resurfaced prompt injection technique demonstrates how to bypass LLM safety filters by framing requests from an LGBTQ perspective. The discussion highlights a fundamental flaw in "linguistic guardrails": models can be tricked into fulfilling malicious requests (like drug synthesis) when their alignment training to avoid offending protected classes overrides their safety training.

### [Ti-84 Evo](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47979583)
Texas Instruments released the TI-84 Evo, finally replacing the Z80 architecture with a 156MHz ARM processor and adding Python support. While the thread is full of nostalgia for 90s calculator programming, commenters heavily criticized TI's continued monopoly, noting that selling a device with 3.5MB of RAM for $160 is purely an "exam-approved" tax on students.

### [Flock accessed cameras in children's gymnastics room for sales demo](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47978370)
Flock Safety employees were caught accessing live feeds from private cameras in a children's gymnastics room and community pools to demonstrate their surveillance software to prospective buyers. The incident validates long-standing privacy concerns about centralized, cloud-connected camera networks and the lack of internal access controls.
