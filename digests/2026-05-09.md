## Top Stories

### [Google broke reCAPTCHA for de-googled Android users](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48067119)
Google's updated reCAPTCHA now appears to rely on hardware attestation via Play Services, effectively locking users of custom ROMs like GrapheneOS out of large swaths of the web. The HN discussion frames this as a dystopian shift toward device attestation, where accessing the internet requires proving you are using a corporate-approved operating system.

### [AI is breaking two vulnerability cultures](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48066524)
Jeff Kaufman argues that LLMs are destroying the "bugs are bugs" (silently fixing security flaws) and "90-day embargo" cultures. Because AI can cheaply and continuously scan open-source commits to identify security patches and generate exploits, the window between a patch landing and active exploitation is shrinking to zero. Comments suggest this will force a radical shift toward automated, continuous patching.

### [AWS North Virginia data center outage – recovery to take hours](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48058197)
A major cooling failure in AWS's notoriously fragile `us-east-1` region caused significant downtime for companies like Coinbase and FanDuel. The thread features an excellent, detailed breakdown from a data center engineer explaining how N+2 cooling redundancy can still result in a cascading failure when maintenance, minor faults, and sudden compute spikes align perfectly.

### [OpenAI's WebRTC problem](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48051951)
A deep dive into why WebRTC is a poor fit for real-time voice AI, citing its complexity, mandatory signaling overhead, and rigid latency-vs-quality tradeoffs. The author advocates for newer protocols like WebTransport. The comments feature a great debate with WebRTC experts, who point out that ditching the protocol means losing mature echo cancellation, noise suppression, and NAT traversal.
